About me

I was born in Virginia, US and moved to Hyderabad, India where I did my entire schooling from the age of five. I am a rising junior at Stanford University where I am majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Theatre - check out my coursework - and play for Stanford's Division 1 varsity squash team.

Growing up, I've always erred on the experimental side and dabbled in activities from skiing and carpentry to abacus and Kuchipudi. By the time I hit double digits though, I was singularly focused on swimming. After years of sweat, determination and hard work from both me and my dad, we won three gold at the Indian Junior Nationals 2013 in Goa. By that time though, I'd already started playing squash during the swimming off season and came second at the squash nationals in the same year. We also have a cameo of racquetball in 2014 where I had the opportunity to represent India at the World Juniors in Colombia. Yup - South America - super dope. 2014 had me balancing all three sports before I doubled down on just squash. Post that, I won the Indian Junior Nationals twice (watch my second national title win here!), finished third at the US Junior Open and was Asia's #1 ranked player in the Under-19's before heading to college!

Outside of the sports world, theatre and student leadership were my secondary extra-curriculars in school. Now in college, I'm co-president of Asha Stanford - a non-profit club that raises over $250,000 annually to fund education projects for underprivileged groups in India. Previously, I served as a board member on the Asian American Theatre Project and the International Undergraduate Community while I was a developer for Stanford's CodeTheChange and a contributing writer to the Stanford Daily.

Technology itself is something that wiggled into my life from the swimming era. It started with using a heart rate monitor connected to the ear and it gradually graduated to an interest in the broad field of consumer tech. I see myself working in areas that combine the power of empathy and a knack to weave new digital experiences into everyday items to give the power to do more and be more. Google's Jacquard summarizes my vision in the tech industry perfectly and concisely — Connected, not distracted.

Currently learning outside school: Hindi, Crossplatform App Dev, Computational Education.
